CS Rep Away Mode

Set yourself Away when you're stepping away from Intercom so tickets don't sit waiting on a rep who's gone quiet.

Turning it on — tap a step

Always pair the two toggles

Away mode alone just changes your status dot. Turn on Reassign replies too, or your tickets will keep waiting on you instead of going to someone who's online.

Coming back for your shift

Turn both toggles back off — Away mode and Reassign replies — and confirm your profile icon has switched from yellow back to green. That's your signal to teammates that you're online and ready for tickets again.

Reply, Snooze & Close

Every reply follows the same shape — a greeting, the answer, and a thank you. Then decide what happens next: snooze it, or close it out.

The structure — tap a step

What makes a reply good

Personal

Use the user's first name when you can find or locate it — it reads human, not automated.

Clear

Answer the actual question first, then add context. Skip jargon and internal shorthand the user won't recognize.

Warm

Match your tone to theirs — friendly for routine questions, direct and empathetic for anyone frustrated or distressed.

Complete

Read the whole thread before replying, so you're not asking for something they already told you.

Proofread before you send

Macros make replies fast, not robotic. Read it back like you're the user receiving it, and personalize anything that still sounds generic.

Snoozing or closing the conversation

Once your reply is sent, decide what happens next — snooze it if you're waiting on the user or another team, or close it out if the issue is resolved.

Snooze (moon icon)

Use when you're waiting on the user to reply, or on another team. Pick a preset (Later today, Tomorrow, Monday, One week, One month) or type a custom time like "1 hour" or "1 day" — the ticket reopens automatically then.

Close

Use once the conversation has ended and the user's issue is resolved. Send your closing message first — closing doesn't send anything to the user on its own.
