Mechanics & Rules

The 30-second version: Splash Marketplace lets users buy and sell pieces of their Survivor contest entries before a contest ends — like a stock market for contest entries. Here's how it actually works.

Fractional ownership

Buy or sell a whole entry or a slice of one — minimum trade is 1%.

Majority rule

Someone must always own more than 50%. That person makes the picks.

Trading window

The market isn't always open — it freezes before each pick lock and reopens right after.

V1 scope

Survivor pools only at launch. Pick X, Tiers, One & Done come later.

The three rules that drive every ticket — tap a card

Path 1 — List & offer

Path 2 — Sell Now / direct offers

Direct offers

A buyer can make an offer directly to any shareholder, even if that person hasn't listed anything. "Trading Partners" (market makers, via API) place standing buy offers across many entries to keep the market liquid.

Sell Now

A seller sees the best standing offer on their entry and can accept it with one tap. If that offer is 20%+ below the entry's Splash Estimate, they get a warning — but they're never blocked from selling. If no standing offers exist, "Sell Now" is hidden and "List for Sale" shows instead.

Don't market this as a guarantee

The Splash Estimate (prize pool ÷ entries remaining) is a simple valuation guide, cached and refreshed after weekly scoring — not a guaranteed price. Sell Now offers 20%+ below it trigger a warning but are never blocked.

The one rule that matters most

One person must own more than 50% of every entry, at all times. That person makes the picks — and if a sale ever flips who holds the majority, pick control transfers automatically. It's never optional and never delayed.
